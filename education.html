Additionally, while studying at the University, I also worked on my own geology honors thesis research. During my research, I gathered data from fossilized stromatolite rock outcroppings in both Colorado and Wyoming. While working on the research, I was advised by both Dr. James Hagadorn (Currator of the Geology Collection at the Denver Museum of Nature and Science) and Dr. Stephen Mojzsis (University of Colorado, Planetary Geology Dept.). After writing my thesis, I then defended it infront of a panel of respected scientists in both the geology and biology fields. My panel consisted of: Dr. James Hagadorn (Currator of the Geology Collection at the Denver Museum of Nature and Science) and Dr. Stephen Mojzsis (University of Colorado, Planetary Geology Dept.), Dr. Charles Stern (University of Colorado, Mineralogy Dept.), and Dr. Noah Fierer (University of Colorado, Biology Dept.). After sucessfully defending my thesis, I was awarded Cum Laude honors and my paper was published by the University of Colorado honors journal.
